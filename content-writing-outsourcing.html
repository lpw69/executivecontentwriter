What is Content Writing Outsourcing?

Content writing outsourcing is the practice of hiring external professional writers to create content for your business instead of using in-house employees. This strategic approach allows companies to access specialized expertise, reduce costs, and scale content production without the overhead of full-time staff.

Rather than hiring full-time content writers, businesses can work with freelance writers, content agencies, or specialized content providers to handle their blog posts, social media content, newsletters, white papers, and other marketing materials.

Benefits of Outsourcing Content Writing

Content writing outsourcing offers numerous advantages for businesses of all sizes. Here are the key benefits that make outsourcing an attractive option:

Cost Effectiveness

The most immediate benefit of content writing outsourcing is cost reduction. Hiring full-time content writers involves salaries, benefits, office space, equipment, and management overhead. Outsourcing eliminates these expenses while often providing better results.

"We reduced our content creation costs by 55% when we switched from in-house writers to outsourcing, while actually improving our content quality and production speed." - Marketing Director, SaaS Company

Access to Specialized Expertise

Outsourced content writers often specialize in specific industries or content types. This means you can access writers who understand your sector, audience, and content requirements better than generalist in-house employees.

Scalability and Flexibility

Content needs fluctuate based on campaigns, seasons, and business priorities. Outsourcing allows you to scale content production up or down instantly without hiring or firing employees.

Faster Time to Market

Professional content writers can start producing content immediately, without the weeks or months required for hiring and onboarding internal staff.

No Management Overhead

Outsourced writers manage themselves, eliminating the need for day-to-day supervision, performance reviews, and administrative tasks associated with employees.

Types of Content to Outsource

Not all content is equally suited for outsourcing. Here's a breakdown of content types that work well with outsourced writers:

Highly Suitable for Outsourcing

  • Blog posts and articles: SEO-focused content that drives traffic
  • Social media content: Regular posts for LinkedIn, Twitter, etc.
  • Email newsletters: Regular subscriber communications
  • Product descriptions: E-commerce and catalog content
  • White papers: Research-based thought leadership
  • Case studies: Customer success stories

Moderately Suitable for Outsourcing

  • Website copy: Requires brand understanding but can work well
  • Press releases: Needs company knowledge but outsourceable
  • Technical documentation: Requires subject matter expertise
  • Sales materials: Needs deep product understanding

Less Suitable for Outsourcing

  • Internal communications: Requires company culture knowledge
  • Crisis communications: Needs immediate, sensitive handling
  • Highly confidential content: Security and privacy concerns
  • Executive communications: Requires personal voice and vision

How to Find Outsourced Content Writers

Finding the right outsourced content writers is crucial for success. Here are the best approaches:

Freelance Platforms

Pros: Large talent pool, competitive pricing, built-in project management

Cons: Quality varies widely, high turnover, less personal relationships

Best for: One-off projects, testing writers, budget-conscious projects

Content Agencies

Pros: Professional processes, quality assurance, account management

Cons: Higher costs, less direct control, potential for less personalized service

Best for: Large-scale content needs, ongoing campaigns, hands-off management

Independent Professional Writers

Pros: Specialized expertise, personal relationships, consistent quality

Cons: Limited capacity, higher individual costs, finding qualified candidates

Best for: Specialized content, long-term partnerships, high-quality requirements

Best Practices for Content Outsourcing

Successful content outsourcing requires clear processes and communication. Follow these best practices:

1. Develop Clear Brand Guidelines

Create comprehensive brand guidelines including:

  • Brand voice and tone specifications
  • Style guide with preferred terminology
  • Content format requirements
  • SEO guidelines and keyword targets
  • Examples of excellent content

2. Establish Clear Communication Protocols

  • Define project timelines and deadlines
  • Set up regular check-in schedules
  • Establish preferred communication channels
  • Create feedback and revision processes
  • Document approval workflows

3. Start with Small Test Projects

Before committing to large projects:

  • Assign 1-2 small pieces to evaluate quality
  • Test communication and revision processes
  • Assess deadline adherence
  • Evaluate cultural and brand fit

4. Provide Comprehensive Briefs

Each content brief should include:

  • Target audience description
  • Content objectives and goals
  • Key messages and talking points
  • Required format and length
  • SEO requirements and target keywords
  • Reference materials and research
  • Deadline and review timeline

Common Outsourcing Mistakes to Avoid

Learning from common mistakes can save time and ensure better results:

1. Choosing Based on Price Alone

The cheapest option rarely provides the best value. Consider quality, reliability, and long-term partnership potential alongside pricing.

2. Insufficient Onboarding

Failing to properly introduce writers to your brand, audience, and expectations leads to poor initial results and longer ramp-up times.

3. Lack of Clear Expectations

Vague briefs and unclear requirements result in content that misses the mark and requires extensive revisions.

4. No Quality Control Process

Without established review and approval processes, inconsistent quality and off-brand content can damage your reputation.

5. Over-Managing the Process

Micromanaging experienced writers reduces efficiency and can damage working relationships. Trust professionals to do their work.

6. Ignoring Cultural Fit

Technical skills matter, but cultural alignment and understanding of your business context are equally important for long-term success.

Pro Tip: The 30-60-90 Day Evaluation

Evaluate new outsourced writers at 30, 60, and 90 days. This allows time for adjustment while catching issues early. Look for improvement in brand voice, reduced revision needs, and increased efficiency over time.

Conclusion

Content writing outsourcing offers significant benefits for businesses looking to scale their content marketing efforts cost-effectively. When done correctly, outsourcing can reduce costs by 40-60% while improving content quality and production speed.

Whether you choose freelance platforms, content agencies, or independent professional writers, focus on quality, reliability, and cultural fit over price alone. With the right approach, content writing outsourcing can become a strategic advantage that drives your content marketing success.
